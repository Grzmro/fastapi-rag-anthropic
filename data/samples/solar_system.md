# The Solar System — Quick Facts

## The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star that contains about 99.86% of the
total mass of the Solar System. Its surface temperature is approximately
5,500 degrees Celsius, while the core reaches around 15 million degrees
Celsius. Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach
Earth.

## Inner planets

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the
Sun, completing an orbit every 88 Earth days. Venus is the hottest planet,
with a surface temperature of about 465 degrees Celsius caused by a runaway
greenhouse effect in its dense carbon dioxide atmosphere. Earth is the only
known planet to support life, and about 71% of its surface is covered by
water. Mars, known as the Red Planet, hosts Olympus Mons — the largest
volcano in the Solar System, standing about 22 kilometers tall.

## Outer planets

Jupiter is the largest planet, with a mass more than twice that of all the
other planets combined. Its Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been
observed for over 300 years. Saturn is famous for its ring system, which is
made mostly of ice particles with a smaller amount of rocky debris. Uranus
rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. Neptune has the
strongest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds of up to 2,100
kilometers per hour.

## Dwarf planets and small bodies

Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International
Astronomical Union. The asteroid belt lies between the orbits of Mars and
Jupiter, and Ceres is the largest object within it. The Kuiper Belt extends
beyond Neptune and contains many icy bodies, including Pluto itself.
